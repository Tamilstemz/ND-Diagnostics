Privacy Policy

Last Updated on: 14th June 2025

At ND Diagnostics India Pvt. Ltd. (“ND Diagnostics”, “we”, “us”, or “our”), protecting the privacy of our customers is of paramount importance. This Privacy Policy (“Policy”) outlines how we collect, store, use, disclose, and transfer your personal and sensitive personal data in compliance with applicable Indian data privacy laws including the Information Technology Act, 2000 and associated rules.

We encourage you to review this page periodically to stay informed about any changes. We value your trust and therefore maintain the highest standards of security for your personal information.

This Policy applies to all individuals availing visa medical services for Australia or interacting with our website, digital interfaces, mobile applications, and physical facilities operated by ND Diagnostics across India (“Services”).

By accessing our services or providing your personal data, you consent to the practices described in this Policy. We will not disclose or sell any of your personal information—including name, address, age, sex, or medical history—to any third party without your permission.

While we use cookie technology to help users navigate our site efficiently, we never use cookies to store personal information such as credit card numbers. Our site may contain links to other websites whose privacy practices may differ. Please email us at info@ndhealthcheck.com for any privacy-related concerns.

1. Definitions

  • Personal Data: Information relating to an identified or identifiable individual, such as name, date of birth, contact details, and passport information.
  • Sensitive Personal Data or Information (SPDI): Health status, biometric data, medical history, etc., as per the IT Rules, 2011.
  • Data Subject / You: The individual whose data is processed by ND Diagnostics.
  • Processing: Any operation on personal data including collection, storage, use, sharing, or deletion.
  • Third Parties: Government agencies, consular entities, diagnostic labs, logistics partners, and service providers.

3. Purpose of Data Collection

ND Diagnostics collects and processes your data to provide and improve our services.

4. Types of Data Collected

4.1 Personal Data

  • Full name, gender, age, nationality
  • Contact details: phone number, email, residential address
  • Passport or travel document details

4.2 Sensitive Personal Data

  • Medical history and diagnostic results
  • Biometric data (e.g., fingerprint, photographs)

4.3 Transactional and Usage Data

  • Payment method and transaction records
  • IP address, device info, cookies

5. Legal Basis for Processing

  • Consent: When explicitly given.
  • Legal Obligations: Compliance with regulations.
  • Contractual Necessity: Processing required to fulfill contracts or pre-contractual steps.

6. Data Sharing and Disclosures

We may share your data with:

  • Government and immigration authorities (e.g., Australian consulates)
  • Partner diagnostic labs
  • Payment gateways and banks
  • IT service providers
  • Legal/regulatory bodies as required by law

We do not sell or rent personal data to third parties for marketing.

8. Security Measures

We implement safeguards including:

  • Data encryption (in transit and at rest)
  • Role-based access control
  • Secure servers and firewalls
  • Regular audits and assessments

No method is 100% secure. You accept this inherent risk by using our services.

9. Your Rights

Under Indian law, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Request corrections
  • Withdraw consent
  • Request data portability
  • Request deletion (with exceptions)

To exercise these rights, contact the Data Protection Officer below.

10. Cookies and Tracking

We use cookies to personalize and improve user experience. You may disable them in browser settings, but some features may not work properly.

11. Cross-Border Data Transfers

Data may be transferred to entities outside India as required for service delivery, with adequate protections in place.

13. Updates to This Policy

This Policy may be updated periodically. Continued use implies acceptance of changes.

14. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Policy is governed by Indian law. Disputes will be resolved under the jurisdiction of courts in New Delhi.
